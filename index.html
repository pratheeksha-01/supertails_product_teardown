Where the experience breaks down

The ultimate trust killer for pet parents who run out of food, forcing them to open Blinkit or Zepto instead.

Poor Support

Customer service is not good and users are highly dissatisfied with the way issues are resolved. Reaching out for delayed orders or wrong items feels like shouting into a void.

Unresolved issues destroy brand loyalty.

Vet Trust Deficit

Users have found that many of the tele-consult Vets were not up to standard. Recent community PSAs on Reddit highlight grave concerns regarding incorrect medical advice, hurting the core telehealth offering.

Medical trust is hard to win back.

Proposed Fixes

Tackling the Trust Deficit: Support, Delivery, and Vet Quality

  • AI Support Chatbot & Supercash

    Integrating an AI chatbot would help customers instantly, saving the company money while perfectly resolving common issues.

    Proactive Resolution: If the delivery is taking more time, the AI chatbot autonomously detects the delay and provides "Super Cash" to the user, shifting the narrative from "They failed" to "They compensated me."

  • Vet Credibility & Transparency

    To fix the Vet quality trust issue, we must prominently share the specific years of experience the vets have and their exact degrees (e.g., BVSc & AH) on their profile cards before booking to regain trust from the user.

  • Delivery Promise Score

    Dynamic calculation based on user pin code, local warehouse stock, and courier metrics to set accurate expectations at checkout.
